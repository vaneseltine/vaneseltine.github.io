I am Research Manager of the Criminal Justice Administrative Records System, a project in the University of Michigan's Institute for Social Research.

Formerly

Last summer, I was a Research Fellow at the National Archive of Criminal Justice Data, part of the International Consortium for Political and Social Research. Before that, an Assistant Professor of Sociology at Bowling Green State University.
I received my Ph.D. in Sociology—with a minor in Social Thought—and my M.A. in Crime, Law, & Justice from The Pennsylvania State University. At Beloit College, I earned my B.A. in both Philosophy and Sociology.
