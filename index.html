Matt VanEseltine

Sociologist, Programmer, Data Enthusiast

I am at the Institute for Research on Science and Innovation at the University of Michigan Institute for Social Research. IRIS is a national consortium of research universities organized around an administrative data repository. We develop data to understand, explain, and improve the public value of higher education.

Formerly

Prior to my current position, I was the research manager for the pilot of the Criminal Justice Administrative Records System in the Population Studies Center at the University of Michigan I spent about five years as an Assistant Professor of Sociology at Bowling Green State University specializing in life-course criminology.
I received my Ph.D. in Sociology—with a minor in Social Thought—from The Pennsylvania State University, following an M.A. in Crime, Law, and Justice. At Beloit College, I earned my B.A. in Philosophy and Sociology.

Also

On the side, I contribute to open source projects in Python. Recently, this has included aiohttp (the top-notch lightweight http framework), nox (testing automation like a better version of tox), python-nameparser (the best available name part parser in Python), and questionary (an outstanding tool to create interactive command-line interfaces).
